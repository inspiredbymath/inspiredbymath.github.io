The Fibonacci Connection

This problem follows the Fibonacci sequence! For N steps, the number of ways equals the (N+1)th Fibonacci number. Here's why: To reach step N, you either came from step N-1 (taking 1 step) or from step N-2 (taking 2 steps). So: ways(N) = ways(N-1) + ways(N-2).

Pattern: 1 step → 1 way (F₂) | 2 steps → 2 ways (F₃) | 3 steps → 3 ways (F₄) | 4 steps → 5 ways (F₅) | 5 steps → 8 ways (F₆) | 6 steps → 13 ways (F₇)
